Drinks

Most college students drink beer and cheap alcohol. I like to be a bit more dignified with my choices in what I drink! Here are some of my go-to drink recipes for any night of the week. Most of my favorite drink recipes contain ingredients that almost all college students have in their apartment. When making drink recipes I try to keep in mind what is going to taste great and look really cool! As long as your drink responsibility, any of these drinks can ensure a fun night!

Corona Sunrise

This drink has no relation to Covid-19 and only to Corona Extra beer. This is one of my go-to drinks that is always a crowd-pleaser. If you have tequila, orange juice, a bottle of Corona, and some limes you can make this drink! Perfect for a day on the beach or your night-time happy hour, anytime is a good time for a Corona. First, grab your Corona and take a nice long sip. Drink the beer until the level is even with the top of the label on the bottle.

How to Make a Corona Sunrise

Next, grab your tequila and fill it up to about where the bottle’s neck begins to narrow. Pour orange juice to the top of the bottle, leaving a little room for a splash of grenadine. Add a splash of grenadine to make the Corona bottle completely full again. Garnish with a wedge of lime and enjoy! This super easy drink requires very few ingredients and always hits the spot. Happy drinking!

A Dirty Shirley

This is the adult version of the Shirley Temple from your childhood! Still just as tasting but with a spin. This drink is easy to make and requires very few ingredients. Whenever I drink this, it takes me back to my childhood. I recommend starting off your night with this drink. I typically only would have one in a given night. This drink is a tooth-ache waiting to happen. Let me take you back to your childhood with this simple recipe.

How to Make a Dirty Shirley

First, grab a glass and fill it to the brim with ice. This glass will be what you are drinking out of so pick accordingly. Add two 1 ½ ounce shots of vodka. Any vodka works! Feeling adventurous? Opt to use a cherry flavored vodka.Next, add three ounces of grenadine. Add more or less to taste.Pour lemon-lime soda into the glass until it is full. Use your favorite brand of lemon-lime soda. I recommend Sprite, or 7UP. Lastly, stir with a spoon or straw and garnish with a maraschino cherry. You have now reentered your childhood! Enjoy!

The Hangover

Nobody ever wants a hangover, but this drink will ensure you have the best night ever. This drink requires a few easy-to-find things you may need to pick up from the store. Anyone can chef this drink to perfection. This drink tastes like the pink lemonade of everyone’s childhood. No one will even know they are drinking alcohol. How to Make the Hangover

Find a cocktail shaker and fill it halfway with ice. First, squeeze half a lemon into the shaker. (I also sometimes use pre-bottled lemon juice and pour for approximately 3 seconds.) Add three 1 ½ ounce shots of Pink Whitney New Amsterdam Vodka. Poor grapefruit juice in for three seconds into the shaker. Cranberry Kamikaze

Meet my signature drink! Impress your friends with this great-tasting shot. They won’t even believe it’s alcohol. Go grab your drink shaker and whip this up for your next Friday night out. Whether for a night in with your friends or a night on the town, these are the perfect shots to make for you and your friends. This classic combination of vodka, triple sec, lime, and cranberry is such a fun way to change up your boring shot of Tito’s. Keep reading to find out how to make the Cranberry Kamikaze!

How to Make the Cranberry Kamikaze

Grab your cocktail shaker and fill it about half-way with ice. (I have included exact measurements but you can also estimate). Squeeze 1 ½ ounces of fresh lime juice into the shaker.Add 1 ½ ounces of cranberry vodka. Pour 1 ounce of triple sec into the mixer. Lastly, add 1 ounce of cranberry Juice. Once you have all your ingredients in the shaker, shake for approximately 20 seconds to chill the shot. Next, strain the mixture into four separate shot glasses! Place a wedge of lime on the rim of the shot glass to finish. Happy drinking!
